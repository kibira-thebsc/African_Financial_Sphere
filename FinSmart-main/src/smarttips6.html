Car : Asset or Liability

Asset is as something you own that can provide future economic benefits.
Liability is where money that must be paid or services that must be performed.
If you purchase a car and use it
For commercial purpose (like Taxi or business) and generate revenue it is an asset.
For moving from point A to point B, which can be done through public transport, it is a liability since it feeds on fuel and requires maintenance.
For moving from point A to point B, which can also be done through public transport but it costs yu large amount of time, it is an asset.
For improving social status without any economic benefit then again liability.
For improving social status which brings economic benefit, in form of important contacts/ Information or business then it is an asset.
If you have multiple wheels and you only drive one, then it is liability(depriciating asset) because it depreciates over time and requires maintenance.

Why you shouldn't buy New Car

For one simple reason , car is a depreciating asset . The one who buys the car brand new (first owner ) have to take biggest cost . Not only he loses on registration , road tax etc but the moment car rolls out of the showroom it loses 20–25% of its because it will be second hand for the next person who will buy it . Buying new car onlys makes sense when you register it on business name so the depreciation part is taken care off or your annual running is enough to compensate the loss .

For example , consider you are new driver who just learnt how to drive but isnt confident . You have 2 options either to buy a low budget hatchback for 5–6 lakh rupees atleast or you can go for a second hand well maintained car which offers you the most value for money . A new Hyundai Verna costs around 12 lakhs , a 3 year old will be available at 6–7 lakhs and a 5 year old will be available for 4–4.5 lakhs . Not only you can get a higher segement car but also get better value for your money .
